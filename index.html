Week 3

The Forbidden Island. A place shrouded in mystery. We arrived in search of fantastic treasures, having heard tales of the splendor to be found on this foreboding piece of the world. Our group of glory seekers knew we had to work together; only with all four working in tandem could we find this island's Shangri-La. First on our team, the brave adventurer Clayton "Goldheart" Shepard, the man with the instincts of a jungle cat, and the strength of a mountain bear. Then, our messenger Davros Swiftfoot. Never has there been a faster man than Swiftfoot, over any terrain, on any continent. Then, the beautiful diving expert Dr Charlotte Darkwater. She knows no fear, and the only match to her beauty is her stunning intellect. Finally, our engineer Bjornn Hardhammer rounds out the group. Though he's a prickly old man, his technical proficiencies are second to none. The island had become unstable, preparing to sink to the cold ocean floor. Time was of the essence, and in order to find the great four treasures of the verboten land, we needed to find the keys. 4 keys to each lock, and 4 locks to be opened to find our golden payday. I tore mine, the Shining Griffin from the claws of the temple of the Stone Jaguar, atop a great peak. Swiftfoot, agile as a gazelle, ran across falling pillars, vaulting an enormous chasm to snatch the Everburning Jewel from its ancient home. As the island sank, it nearly took him with it, but his impeccable balance and perfect strides kept him just far enough ahead to outstep the catastrophe. Meanwhile, deep below, the beautiful doctor dove deep into the ancient caverns below the island, looking for the Kraken's Goblet. She found it nestled between the teeth of some long dead ancient leviathan, far beyond the territory of man. Even the unshakable heart of this marine expert felt unsettled at the size of the gargantuan beast. Finally, in the mountains of the Abbadon, Hardhammer faced a challenge of his own. In order to capture the Demon's Egg, he had a river of lava to cross, with only stone and rope to build a bridge. Any other man would have quailed at such a task, but our genius engineer felt no fear, merely mild annoyance that none would see this creation. The bridge lasted just long enough to see him safely back before the lava consumed it, melting away any trace that man had conquered it's trap. Racing to Fool's Landing, we four heroes barely reached the pickup point before the very rock below us crumbled into the unforgiving ocean. As the helicopter carried us to safety, I lit up a cigar, and smiled. "It ain't easy being cheesy"
